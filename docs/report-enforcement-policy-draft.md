# Report and Enforcement Policy Draft

Last updated: 2026-04-22
Status: internal release draft for legal review

## 1. Purpose

This draft describes how Neonadri may receive, review, and act on reports and other safety or moderation signals.

## 2. Reportable issues

Reports or internal moderation flags may cover:

- unsafe payment requests
- payment for attendance, time, or companionship
- harassment or threatening behavior
- misleading meetup content
- suspected underage access
- repeated abuse, evasion, or account misuse

## 3. Intake sources

Signals may come from:

- user-submitted reports where supported in the product
- blocking activity or safety actions
- internal moderation rules and pattern flags
- admin review
- legal or third-party complaints

## 4. Review process

Moderation review may consider:

- the report text and attached detail
- meetup content and related profile information
- match and chat context where relevant
- prior account history and repeat patterns
- urgency and severity of potential harm

## 5. Possible actions

Neonadri may:

- leave content unchanged
- warn the user
- remove or restrict content
- hide a meetup from discovery
- limit account features
- suspend or terminate the account
- preserve records for legal, safety, or enforcement needs

## 6. High-priority cases

The following should receive priority review:

- threats or coercion
- sexual or romantic compensation requests
- repeated payment-for-attendance language
- suspected minors using the service
- serious safety complaints tied to an in-person meetup

## 7. Documentation

Moderation decisions should be documented with enough detail to explain:

- what triggered review
- what evidence was considered
- what action was taken
- whether follow-up is needed

## 8. Appeals and discretion

If an appeal path is added, it should be documented separately. Until then, Neonadri may resolve enforcement decisions in its reasonable discretion, especially in safety-sensitive cases.
