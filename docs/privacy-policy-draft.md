# Privacy Policy Draft

Last updated: 2026-04-22
Status: internal release draft for legal review

## 1. Overview

This draft explains what Neonadri collects, why it is collected, how it is used, and how long it may be retained.

Neonadri is designed to help adults discover and coordinate in-person meetups.

## 2. Information we collect

### Information you provide

We may collect:

- account information such as email address
- profile information such as display name, about me, gender, age group, languages, interests, meeting style, and response note
- meetup content such as purpose, place name, location, meetup time, duration, target preferences, and cost support amount
- messages and reviews you send through the service
- support, safety, moderation, and report submissions

### Information created through use of the service

We may collect:

- match request history
- match status and chat activity metadata
- review history and trust-related signals
- block and safety events
- account and session activity relevant to service security and abuse prevention

### Location information

Neonadri may process location-related information such as:

- place names and addresses entered by users
- map selections and map-based coordinates used to position meetups
- approximate user-device location when used for feed or map relevance, where available and permitted

### Technical information

We may collect basic technical and usage information such as:

- browser and device metadata
- IP address or network-derived region information
- timestamps and request logs
- cookies or similar session information used for authentication and product operation

## 3. How we use information

We may use information to:

- create and manage accounts
- display profiles, meetups, and match flows
- support feed ranking, map placement, and meetup discovery
- enable chat, requests, reviews, and related product features
- reduce abuse, fraud, and safety risk
- investigate reports and enforce platform rules
- improve product performance and reliability
- comply with legal obligations

## 4. Cost support and safety use

We may review meetup or profile information to detect unsafe or prohibited cost support behavior, including attempts to use the product to offer money for attendance, time, companionship, or personal interaction.

## 5. How location information is handled

Neonadri may reduce the precision of location information before a match is confirmed. Exact or more detailed location information may be shown later in the user flow where product rules allow it.

## 6. Sharing

We may share information:

- with service providers that help us operate the platform
- with mapping, hosting, messaging, and infrastructure vendors
- when required by law or legal process
- when necessary to protect safety, rights, or platform integrity
- in connection with a merger, acquisition, restructuring, or sale of assets

We do not need to publicly expose all information we process. Some information is used internally for safety, moderation, fraud prevention, or account operations.

## 7. User controls

Depending on product availability and applicable law, users may be able to:

- update profile information
- change their password
- block other users
- delete their account

Some records may remain where retention is necessary for legal, security, moderation, or safety reasons.

## 8. Retention

Retention is described in more detail in the Data Retention / Deletion Policy draft. In general, we retain information for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the service, protect the platform, investigate abuse, and comply with legal obligations.

## 9. Security

We use administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to protect information, but no system can guarantee absolute security.

## 10. Children

Neonadri is for adults 18+ only and is not intended for children.

## 11. California and other privacy rights

Depending on applicable law, users may have rights to request access, correction, or deletion of certain personal information. Final published rights language should be aligned with counsel review and the product's operational capabilities.

## 12. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The effective version should be the one posted with the latest update date.
